  eCRE Exam Review

        December 13, 2021

   Hi again Tim!  It's been a stunning year since my last blog post and I'm making it up with a review of eLearnSecurity's Certified Reverse Engineer (eCRE) exam since I recently passed!  eCRE is the exam taken after completing the Reverse Engineering Professional (REP) course from INE/eLearnSecurity.  From their certification page, a student can expect to learn the following:

  • x86 32-bit Architecture
  • Windows Portable Executable (PE) Format
  • Analyzing contents of the stack
  • Analyzing detailed algorithms
  • Anti-debugging techniques (and how to bypass)
  • Obfuscation mechanisms (and how to bypass them)
  • Performing manual unpacking
  • Analyzing multi-threaded binaries

Preparation

   The instructor covers the material entirely in a Windows XP Virtual Machine (VM).  This is not required and Windows 7 works perfectly fine.  I covered most of the course using FlareVM on Windows 10, but had to switch to Windows 7 for the multithreaded applications lesson due to some Application Programming Interface (API) used.  The primary tools used in the course are:

  • OllyDbg
  • Import Reconstructor
  • OllyDump plugin
You can use other debuggers such as Immunity or x32dbg if you're more comfortable.  They have the same CPU layout as OllyDbg.  After finishing the course material, exam preparation is straightforward.  Ensure you reverse each challenge binary at least twice and get comforatble reversing them without referencing the videos.   Shortly before the exam, I migrated everything over to the Windows 7 VM (titled "Exam") to ensure the smoothest exam environment.  As a bonus, I read the Processes, Threads, and Memory Management chapters of Modern Operating Systems 4th Edition by Andrew Tanenbaum.

Exam Format

   You will get a pdf exam manual after paying for the voucher.  It will provide all instructions needed to execute the exam.


   The eCRE exam is administered in two parts: A multple choice quiz and a hands-on reversing challenge.  Part 1 is the multiple choice quiz.  You need to get an 88% (40/45 questions correct) in 90 minutes to advance to Part 2.  Furthermore, you do NOT get a free retake after failing Part 1; only if you fail Part 2.  This is probably the hardest part of the exam due to the tiny margin of error.  Most exams allow a 70% score to pass.  Not this one!  The exam is open book so it's best to have searchable notes on hand.   Part 2 is the hands-on reversing challenge in which you have 24 hours to analyze a binary and submit a findings report.  Part 2 must begin within 14 days of successfully completing Part 1.

Exam Experience

   Everything covered in REP is in the exam.  Everything.  Expect to see all material in the exam and review any weak areas.  During Part 1 I had notes on my left screen, the exam on my middle screen, and a web browser on my right screen.  In a pinch, I spun up my Exam VM to confirm the output of certain questions, which was very convenient!  Pay attention to the time throughout Part 1 because some questions, like obfuscation, are time consuming.  Although obfuscation is straightforward, it is still time consuming during a 90 minute quiz so make sure you can quickly understand assembly instructions.
   Part 2 begins whenever you want within the 14 day window.
