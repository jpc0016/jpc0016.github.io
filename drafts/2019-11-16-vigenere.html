 The Vigenere Cipher

    Hello, Tim and Aaron!  I got a perfect score on my school assignment so I decided to share it.  I cannot go into anything specific about the class, instructor, or even parts of the program so I'll omit those.   At the high level, it is a generic cybersecurity graduate course with coding assignments as homework.  This particular project is about cracking the Vigenere cipher.

  Quick Primer

    A brief introduction to the Caeser cipher is needed to understand the Vigenere cipher.  The Caeser cipher uses the alphabet as its list of possible values and one number as its key.  So a phrase "I love dogs" with a key of 3 will shift each letter of "ILOVEDOGS" by three to make "LORYHGRJV".  If a letter is shifted after 'Z', it will start from the beginning of the alphabet.  So a character 'Y' with the same key will shift by 3 to make 'B'.  This is a very simple mechanism that is scaled in the Vigenere cipher.
    The Vigenere cipher is an advancement of the Caeser cipher in that it uses a word or phrase as its key.  Instead of shifting every letter in "I love dogs" by a simple number, each letter is shifted based on its position relative to the key.  Let's say the key is "hello", each letter of "hello" would be superimposed on the phrase "I love dogs" and shifted based on the letter value of the key:

I L O V E D O G S H E L L O H E L L + ----------------- P P Z G S K S R D

In the above, 'I' has a value 8 (starting at index 0) and 'H' has a value of 7.
