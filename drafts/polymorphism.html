   This post involves creating polymorphic versions of three shellcode samples from Shell-Storm .  A bit of familiarity is needed before continuing.  The purpose of encoding is to obfuscate shellcode in order to maneuver a payload.  A review of the reverse TCP shell is below:

From the previous article:   Reverse shells are functionally similar to bind shells but work in the opposite direction.  Instead, the attacker machine listens for incoming connections from the vicitim machine over a preconfigured port (such as 4444).  The connection attempt is initiated by the victim machine by invoking a reverse shell.  Below is what a reverse TCP shell looks like.
